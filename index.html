the premise

creationships compound

once a week, the back room of a vegan restaurant on east 6th fills with people who'd never otherwise share a table — a founder, a blacksmith, a prompt engineer, a doula, a jazz pianist, whoever showed up. a few hours later, something exists to make this world better that none of them could've made alone.

we call what happens between them a creationship — the relationship that forms when you make something together. and creationships compound. the person you prototyped with becomes the person you call next month to pick their brain about work, the person you text when you're stuck on the real creative problem, and the person you text at 9am because you're out of eggs.

the world building ahead won't come from solo builders or siloed disciplines. it'll come from networks of people who've already made things together, who share a language, who know how each other builds.

how sundays go

no experience required. no finished product required.

11:50
doors open
grab food, meet people, get oriented.
12:00
community pulse
a knowledge share from someone in the group — how we stay on top of what's shifting in new tech and get to know each other.
12:30
vision casting
everyone shares what they're working on, what they're curious about, or what they'd help with. two minutes each. half-baked is welcome. teams form around what's interesting.
1:00
the build
a few hours of deep, collaborative work. bring your laptop, your sketchbook, your ai tools, your napkin ideas. you are the toolkit.
3:00
show & tell
we share what we made and witness each other. something is alive now that wasn't at noon.
