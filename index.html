the room

we don't curate the guest list. the room takes the shape of whoever shows up.

past weeks brought together a doula, a prompt engineer, a high school teacher, a muralist, and someone restoring old radios. someone teaches a concept they can't stop thinking about. then, we pitch our half-formed projects. teams form naturally around whatever sparks interest. for the rest of the time, we build, we eat, and we share what we made.

we are practicing building new worlds. the world outside is largely playing a finite game — optimizing for extraction, scale, and moving as fast as possible. in this room, we step out of that.

in practice, we are relearning how to cultivate creative attention and trust. we're slowing down to focus fiercely on one thing, alongside people who think completely differently than we do. the work is sitting shoulder-to-shoulder with strangers, sharing our imagination, and building something together.

the relationships that come from this — we call them creationships. the person you build a prototype with over several weeks becomes the person you call at 9 AM because life feels stuck or you're simply out of eggs.

how a sunday goes

no experience required. no finish line.

11:50
doors
grab food, meet people, get oriented.
12:00
someone teaches
a knowledge share from someone in the group — a concept they can't stop thinking about.
12:30
two-minute pitches
everyone shares what they're working on. half-baked is welcome. teams form around what's interesting.
1:00
building
deep, collaborative work. bring your laptop, your sketchbook, your ai tools, your napkin ideas.
3:00
show & tell
we share what we made. something is alive now that wasn't at noon.

you leave inspired, a little changed, and with people you'll see again.
